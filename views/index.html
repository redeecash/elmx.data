Steps to Going Public:

From Startup to Corporate Giant
  1. Register on Exempt Liquidity Market Exchange
  2. Register with the SEC as a Transfer Agent for your own securities
  3. Locate a CPA in good standing
  4. Prepare a Registration for Regulation Section 3(a)(11) intra-state offering
  5. Register Section 3(a)(11) with your home US State
  6. Apply for a CUSIP (and ISIN) for the Section 3(a)(11) offering
  7. Obtain a trading symbol for your Section 3(a)(11) offering on Exempt Liquidity Market Exchange
  8. List yor Section 3(a)(11) offering on Exempt Liquidity Market Exchange
  9. File Quarterly and Annual Reports on Exempt Liquidity Market Exchange for the Section 3(a)(11) offering with the annual report audited by a qualified CPA
  10. Locate a PCAOB registered Auditor in good standing
  11. File a Form 10-12G for the Section 3(a)(11) Offering (will meet reporting requirements, if timely, after 12 months for a simplified S-3 filing)
  12. Maintain your 10-Q, 10-K and 8-K filing compliance (must maintain timely reporting during twelve months to be eligible to use simplified S-3)
  13. Locate a qualified attorney for the S-1(S-3) offering
  14. File a Draft Registration Statement
  15. File an S-1 (or S-3: usable for companies with at least 12 months of timely reporting, if a Form 10 was filed for exempt offering for voluntary reporting, then an S-3 may be used instead of a S-1?) and any amendments
  16. File form 8-A
  17. Obtain a CUSIP and ISIN number for the S-1 (or S-3) offering
  18. Obtain a trading symbol from FINRA for the S-1 (or S-3) offering
  19. Begin the process of a Direct Public Offering on the NYSE, UTP may be used for Section 3(a)(11) conversion to S-1(S-3) shares
  20. Receive a Notice of Effectiveness
  21. File a Prospectus under Rule 424B4
  22. Obtain a CUSIP and ISIN number for any spinoff registrations
  23. File Form 10-12B for any spinoff registrations
